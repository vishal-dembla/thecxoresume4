Why This Works

The traditional resume approach fails C-suite executives because it treats decades of leadership experience like a chronological inventory. But boards, investors, and search partners don’t hire inventory—they hire vision, judgment, and the ability to navigate what’s next. When Maria, a CFO whose company was acquired, first approached us, her resume read like a financial metrics checklist. Her real story, however, was about leading three successful integrations while building teams that outlasted the deals. That narrative, properly positioned, opened conversations about transformation roles that her bullet-point resume never could. We help executives like Maria understand that their resume should frame the future, not just catalog the past.

Who We Work With

Our clients span high-stakes transitions: CXOs navigating layoffs, mergers, or pivots; first-time executives moving into the C-suite; board aspirants; founders post-exit; senior military leaders crossing into enterprise; and non-linear paths that resist templates. What unites them is the need for narrative architecture—not formatting.

Our Process

We begin with a discovery call to map the contours of your leadership story and define the positioning that serves your next chapter. Then a deep-dive interview (often over multiple sessions) surfaces patterns, choices, and inflection points. From there we craft your brand narrative and resume, iterate with structured feedback, and deliver materials that feel unmistakably yours—built for sophisticated readers.

What You Get

A bespoke executive resume that reads like the opening chapter of your next phase, plus a positioning narrative many clients reuse for networking and interviews. Optional add-ons: LinkedIn alignment and board-bio development. Each asset works as part of a coherent leadership brand.

“Your process helped me understand my story. I got two interviews in the first month, but more importantly, I finally knew how to talk about what I bring.”
