The Story Behind the Stories

Why I built a practice around executive narrative clarity.

After watching dozens of exceptional leaders undersell themselves with conventional resume formats, I realized the problem wasn't their careers—it was how they were telling their stories. Traditional resume templates force decades of vision and judgment into bullet points, stripping away the very qualities that make C-suite executives compelling.

My background spans strategy consulting and executive communications, where I learned that the most successful leaders aren't just great operators—they're master storytellers who understand how to frame their experience for maximum impact. Yet when it came to their own career narratives, even the most articulate executives defaulted to chronological lists that buried their strategic value.

The Narrative Clarity Problem

The turning point came while advising a CFO whose company had been acquired. On paper, her resume looked like financial metrics and reporting structures. In conversation, her real story emerged: she had orchestrated three successful integrations, built teams that outlasted every transition, and developed a reputation for turning operational chaos into strategic advantage. That narrative—properly positioned—opened doors to transformation roles that her bullet-point resume never could.

This experience revealed a fundamental gap in how senior executives present themselves. At the C-suite level, hiring decisions aren't about qualifications—they're about judgment, vision, and the ability to navigate what's next. But traditional resumes don't capture these qualities.

"Most leaders spend twenty years building a career and twenty minutes on their resume. The asymmetry is staggering."

Building a Different Process

The CXO Resume emerged from a simple thesis: executive transitions require narrative clarity, not template efficiency. Our discovery process—often spanning multiple sessions—uncovers patterns and themes that even clients haven't recognized in their own careers. We surface the through-lines that connect disparate experiences into coherent leadership narratives.

Every engagement is bespoke because every leadership journey is unique. We work with CEOs pivoting to board roles, founders transitioning after exits, military leaders crossing into enterprise, and established executives whose unconventional paths resist template-driven approaches. What unites them is the need for narrative architecture—not formatting.

Beyond Documents

While we deliver polished resumes, the deeper value lies in helping clients understand their own stories with fresh clarity. This narrative foundation serves far beyond job searching—it informs networking conversations, board presentations, and strategic positioning for years to come. Clients often tell us the discovery process revealed leadership themes they hadn't consciously recognized.

The work is deeply collaborative. I believe the best resume writers are translators, not writers—we help you say what you mean with precision and power. Every word serves your positioning strategy. Every section advances your narrative. Every choice reflects your authentic leadership voice.
